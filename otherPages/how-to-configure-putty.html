    • To change (and save) various Putty preference settings, first type in the“Host Name (or IP address)” of the machine you wish to connect to. In this example we will use monet. For the full list of available Unix servers, please see: http://dscomputing.dfci.harvard.edu/index.php/server-computing/unix-login-servers

    Also type a short name to save it as under “Saved Sessions”:

    Next, Click on “Window” on the left (“Category”) side of the Putty Configuration window to change size of window (rows/columns) from the default (80/24):

    Next, click on “Appearance” to change the font size and style:

    Click “OK” and Click “Colours” to change the foreground and background color. Click “Default Foreground” and then “Modify”:

    Click the Black square under “Custom colors” (or any other color you want to choose):

    Click “OK”. Do the same for “Default Background”, this time choosing the White square and click “OK”:

    Now, on the left side of the putty Configuration window, Click “Session” then “Save”

    You can now (and from now on) double-click on the saved “monet” with your chosen preferences:

    If you want to add to or change any of these preferences in the future, first “Load” the “Saved Session”, make your changes, and re-save.

    To enable X-window applications (emacs, SAS, etc.), click the + sign next to “SSH” in the Putty Configuration window, then click X11 and put a check mark next to “Enable X11 forwarding”. You will also need to have Xming running to get your X11 window:

    Again, scroll back up to “Session” and “Save” if you want the X11 forwarding to be permanent, before clicking “Open” to start the Putty session.
